Abstract

At the present time education has been transforming from its conventional ways to adapt to new environments due to budget, population diversity, political beliefs, new family concepts, and technology. Based on NeaToday (News and features from the National Education Association) in not small changes to adjustment, but a bundle of strategies needed to match with the population that is served at the present time.

The pressure is not just on teachers, but also in students and parents. Students today face extreme pressure, like social media, parents mobility due to job cuts or agricultural cycles, not counting inmigration factors of massive population to a different country, carrying in the kids language disadvantage and psychological effects.

Schools today are adapting to serve better students with mental issues, providing attention and support to those not able to cope with their present situations, including the visual explosion of mass shooting at school, or kids highly depressed at the point to suicide. Chronic absenteeism is another cause for students' problems (more than 8 million kids missed more than 3 weeks of school during 2016), lack of motivation and economic instability that causes students not to succeed in an academic environment.

The No Child Left Behind (NCLB) Act, was enacted in 2002 by President Bush, amendments to this law has been implemented in 2010 by President Obama, where new provisions has been done tNCLB. In 2012, the Obama administration began granting flexibility to states regarding specific requirements of NCLB in exchange for rigorous and comprehensive state- developed plans designed to close achievement gaps, increase equity, improve the quality of instruction, and increase outcomes for all students (US Department of Education). The law protects all students no matter their economic status, religion, race or zip code. In order to accommodate students based on their personal needs, California School Districts have adopted different plans to support their pupils. Still student mobility among schools and school programs is inevitable. In this small study the visualization of mobilization will show us the complexity of the problem.
